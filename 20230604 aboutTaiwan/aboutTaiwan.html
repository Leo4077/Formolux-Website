| Center of Asia  |

Taiwan is the heart of Asia. a perfect starting point for a journey to nearby destinations such as Japan, South Korea, China, and Southeast Asia.
If you're planning to travel around Asia, Taiwan must be at the top of your list.

| The Weather  |

Taiwan has a subtropical climate with temperatures ranging from 20-30°C (68-86°F) throughout the year. Summers are hot and humid, while winters are mild and comfortable, making it an attractive destination for travelers seeking a break from colder climates.

| The Landscape  |

Located at the convergence of the Eurasian Plate and the Philippine Sea Plate, Taiwan has a diverse and stunning landscape that includes towering mountains, verdant forests, majestic waterfalls, stunning coastlines, picturesque valleys, and hot springs.

| The Food  |

Taiwanese cuisine is a vibrant and diverse blend of influences from China, Japan, Southeast Asia, and beyond. From street food to fine dining, Taiwan offers a wide range of delicious dishes, including beef noodle soup, dumplings, and bubble tea.

| The Culture  |

Taiwanese culture is incredibly diverse and rich. With its ancient temples and traditional rituals, as well as the popular fly sky lantern culture, Taiwan has so much to offer. We include them in our travel plans, so you can have the opportunity to experience our culture.

| The People  |

Taiwanese people are known for their friendliness and warmth. They exhibit a strong sense of respect and politeness, treating others with courtesy and showing reverence to elders. With an open and inclusive mindset, they embrace cultural diversity and hold a strong sense of loyalty and unity among their families and friends.

| Useful Information  |

1. 110V/60Hz electrical system
2. The currency is the New Taiwan Dollar (NTD)
3. Major credit cards are accepted, and ATMs are widely available.
4. Taiwan’s official language is Mandarin Chinese, but English can be spoken in most tourist areas.
